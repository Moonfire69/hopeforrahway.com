About Hope

Rahway deserves a mayor who works for you.

I am Hope, artist, grandmother, former EMT, community organizer, and independent candidate for Mayor of Rahway.

I moved to Rahway for the arts district and stayed because this city matters. I have been doing the research, filing OPRA requests, studying the contracts, and asking the hard questions residents deserve answers to.

I am not backed by a party machine. I am backed by the people of Rahway and accountable only to them.

The Five Root Principles

A platform built for Rahway residents.

Every issue on this platform came from listening to Rahway residents. These are not talking points. They are commitments.

01

Municipal Water Control

Rahway water belongs to Rahway residents. We will end opaque water contracts and establish full public transparency over our water system, supply, and safety.

02

Affordable Housing

The promises that drew people to Rahway must be honored. We will fight displacement and demand accountability from developers.

03

Flood Resilience

Climate change is not coming. It is here. Rahway must invest in real flood infrastructure and stop greenwashing projects that do not protect residents.

04

Embedded Mental Health Services

Mental health response belongs in our communities. We will build integrated services that reach residents where they are.

05

Arts District Development

Rahway's creative community is an asset, not an afterthought. We will deliver on the arts district promises and ensure artists can afford to live and work here.

Radical Transparency

You have a right to know.

City government works best when residents are informed. That means publishing meeting materials in advance, posting contracts publicly, and making OPRA requests unnecessary because the information was never hidden in the first place.

As Mayor, I will implement open budget dashboards, publish all vendor contracts, and hold regular public office hours, not just election-year town halls.

Radical transparency is not a risk. It is how trust gets built.
