About Hope

    

A Rahway resident. A community organizer. A candidate.

    

Hope Moran is an artist, grandmother, former EMT, and lifelong community organizer who has called Rahway home since 2018. She has spent her career showing up for people — as a first responder, as Chair of Health and Human Services for the NAACP Rahway Branch, and as a small business owner who built Le Bretzel into one of the city's most beloved restaurants.

    

Now she's running for Mayor as an independent — beholden to no party, no machine, no special interest. Just Rahway.

The Five Root Principles

  

What we're fighting for.

  
    
      

01

      

Municipal Water Control

      

Keep Rahway's water public. No privatization. Full transparency on water quality, contracts, and the Merck contamination at 126 East Lincoln Avenue.

    
    
      

02

      

Affordable Housing

      

Real affordable housing for Rahway residents, not luxury development that prices out the people who built this city.

    
    
      

03

      

Flood Resilience

      

Invest in infrastructure that protects Rahway's neighborhoods from flooding — now, before the next storm.

    
    
      

04

      

Embedded Mental Health

      

Mental health services woven into schools, first response, and community programs — not as an afterthought, but as infrastructure.

    
    
      

05

      

Arts District Development

      

Rahway has a creative community. Build an arts district that supports local artists, drives economic development, and makes the city a destination.
