About Hope

A Life of Service.
A Voice for Rahway.

I have spent my life in service to community. I worked as an EMT for fifteen years, including during September 11, 2001. I worked with the NAACP Rahway Branch on Health and Human Services. I have been politically active since age 16.

I moved to Rahway in 2018 and immediately saw a city full of potential being held back by closed-door governance. I built Le Bretzel, a restaurant in Elizabeth. I built an art practice here. Rahway is my home, and I refuse to watch it be sold off piece by piece while residents are kept in the dark.

I also create mixed media paintings and sculptural jewelry from reclaimed materials through Moonfire Studios. I believe art and civic life are inseparable. Both are acts of radical imagination.

Radical Transparency is a lifestyle for me — not just a policy.

"I've lived my life as an open book. If someone can learn from my mistakes or grow from my experience without having to go through it themselves, why would I keep that to myself? We're all human. We all make mistakes. I'm just honest about mine." — Hope Moran

The Platform

The Five Root Principles

Not promises. Not talking points. A framework built from listening to this community and understanding what Rahway actually needs to grow and thrive for everyone who lives here.

Root 01

Municipal Water Control

Rahway's water belongs to Rahway's residents. Not to a private equity firm. Not to a utility conglomerate. I will fight to keep public water publicly controlled, fully transparent, and accountable to the people who drink it.

Root 02

Affordable Housing

Development should serve the people who already live here. We need real affordability, anti-displacement protections, and housing policy that keeps long-term residents from being priced out of the city they built.

Root 03

Flood Resilience

Rahway's flood risk is rising and our stormwater infrastructure has not kept pace. The Army Corps of Engineers has documented what residents already know: the Rahway River is a threat to homes and neighborhoods. I will prioritize flood resilience, real stormwater management, and a serious response to rising water that protects every part of this city.

Root 04

Embedded Mental Health Services

Police are not mental health workers. We need embedded mental health professionals in our city services, schools, and community response teams so that people in crisis get care, not punishment.

Root 05

Arts District Development

Arts and culture are not decoration. They are economic drivers and community glue. I will champion a real arts district in Rahway that supports local artists, attracts creative businesses, and makes this city a destination.

Why Independent

No Party. No Machine.
Just Rahway.

"Radical transparency is not a slogan. It is a commitment. Every decision I make as Mayor will be made in the open, with the community at the table."

The two-party system in Rahway is not delivering for Rahway residents. When the same political insiders cycle through positions, when party counsel represents both the city and the party in legal matters, when water deals are struck without a disclosed dollar amount or public input, something is broken.

I am not running against a party. I am running for the residents who show up to council meetings with questions and get non-answers. For the people who have watched developers get sweetheart deals while renters get squeezed. For the neighborhoods that flood every time it rains heavily, and have been waiting for solutions for years.

Independent means I answer to you. That is not a talking point. It is the entire point.

Radical Transparency

What Transparency
Actually Looks Like

This campaign operates in public. Here is what that means in practice.

Public Campaign Finance

All donations disclosed. All expenditures disclosed. You can see exactly who is funding this campaign and how every dollar is spent.

Open Research

When I investigate city contracts, property deals, or political conflicts of interest, I share what I find publicly, with sourced documentation.

No Backroom Deals

I will not make private agreements with developers, political donors, or party officials in exchange for support. That is not how this campaign works.

Community First Decisions

Every major policy decision will include public input. Not a checkbox. A real process with real community voice before anything is finalized.
